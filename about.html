About

Theoretical Biology Group

“too mathematical to interest most biologists and not sufficiently mathematical to interest most mathematicians”
J.B.S. Haldane

The University of Chicago,
Department of Ecology & Evolution,
Zoology Building room 403 and 403a,
1101 E. 57th street, 60637 Chicago, IL, USA.

What we do

We develop new mathematical, statistical and computational methods for theoretical ecology. The main areas we cover are:

  • Ecological networks: species in ecological communities form tangled networks of ecological interactions...
  • Stability of large ecological systems: using Random Matrix Theory...
  • Game theoretical models in ecology: many game-theoretical models...
  • Lotka-Volterra dynamics: the Lotka-Volterra equations were proposed more than a century ago...
  • Science of science: can we study the scientific endeavor using the same tools...

Join the laboratory

Some general considerations:

  • Our work is exquisitely theoretical/mathematical/statistical/computational...
  • We typically make use of new, and sometimes innovative methods and tools...
  • We do lots of programming. We mostly use R, python and C...

Graduate students: I accept students through the graduate program in Ecology & Evolution...

Postdocs: I rarely advertise positions...

Research assistants: I typically keep two undergraduate students working in the lab...

Diversity: the laboratory values diversity in all its forms...
